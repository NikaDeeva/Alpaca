Who are alpacas?

Alpaca is a South American domestic animal of the camelid family. Alpacas live in the Andes at an altitude of 3,500-5,000 meters, in the territory of Ecuador, southern Peru, northern Chile and western Bolivia. These creatures were domesticated about 6,000 years ago by the Indians of Peru. First of all, alpacas are valued for their wool, from which blankets, clothing and much more are made.

Why buy an alpaca?

  • Valuable wool

    It is distinguished by its purity, fine fiber and strength.

  • Simple care and maintenance

    It is necessary to provide them with food, water and build a pen with a canopy or provide other shelter (clean, unheated room) in case of bad weather.

  • Alive toy

    Alpacas get along wonderfully with people, can participate in games with disabled children, and brighten up the loneliness of elderly people.

  • Friendly and cute pet

    Alpaca is a cute and funny animal that you can spend time with, forgetting about your problems and worries. By purchasing an alpaca, you acquire a positive friend who can give you a smile every day.

Color range

Alpaca wool comes in 24 natural shades, from pure white, traditional beige or silver to brown and even black.

Hypoallergenic

Alpaca wool fiber does not contain lanolin, making it hypoallergenic.
