Who are alpacas?

Alpaca is a South American domestic animal of the camelid family. Alpacas live in the Andes at an altitude of 3,500-5,000 meters, in the territory of Ecuador, southern Peru, northern Chile and western Bolivia. These creatures were domesticated about 6,000 years ago by the Indians of Peru. First of all, alpacas are valued for their wool, from which blankets, clothing and much more are made.
